## Character Overview & Tier List

### Unlock Order
Ironclad (default) -> Silent (play a run as Ironclad) -> Regent (play a run as Silent) -> Necrobinder (play a run as Regent) -> Defect (play a run as Necrobinder).
You do NOT need to win -- just complete (or abandon) a run.

### Character Rankings (Early Access Meta)

| Tier | Character | Rationale |
|------|-----------|-----------|
| S | The Regent | Dual resource system (Energy + Stars) enables absurd scaling. 75 HP is comfortable. Star payoff cards can one-shot bosses. |
| S | The Silent | Sly keyword is rated S+ tier by community. Damage ceiling is virtually limitless. Poison and Shiv builds remain strong alternatives. |
| A | The Defect | Orb engine is powerful but Focus changes from STS1 make it harder to snowball. Claw deck is a strong alternative. |
| A | The Ironclad | Very consistent. Strongest sustain via Burning Blood. Vulnerable-focused build is new and powerful. Self-damage archetype is now top-tier. |
| B+ | The Necrobinder | Highest skill ceiling. Lowest HP (66). Incredibly strong when piloted correctly but punishes mistakes harshly. |

### Starting Stats

| Character | HP | Gold | Starting Relic | Starting Deck |
|-----------|-----|------|---------------|---------------|
| Ironclad | 80 | 99 | Burning Blood (heal 6 HP after each combat) | 5x Strike, 4x Defend, 1x Bash |
| Silent | 70 | 99 | Ring of the Snake (draw 2 extra cards at combat start) | 5x Strike, 5x Defend, 1x Neutralize, 1x Survivor |
| Defect | 75 | 99 | Cracked Core (channel 1 Lightning Orb at combat start) | 4x Strike, 4x Defend, 1x Zap, 1x Dualcast |
| Regent | 75 | 99 | Divine Right (gain 3 Stars at combat start) | 4x Strike, 4x Defend, 1x Falling Star, 1x Venerate |
| Necrobinder | 66 | 99 | Bound Phylactery (summon Osty at combat start) | 4x Strike, 4x Defend, 1x Bodyguard, 1x Unleash |

## The Ironclad

### Key Changes from STS1
- **Removed cards**: Flex, Heavy Blade, Limit Break, and Reaper are GONE in STS2.
- **New Vulnerable archetype**: Dedicated Vulnerable exploitation is a central pillar now.
- **Self-Damage archetype** is more prominent with new cards like Blood Wall, Breakthrough, Inferno.
- **Corruption** is now only available from a specific Ancient encounter, not in the normal card pool.
- **Dark Embrace** is now Rare (was Uncommon in STS1).
- The Exhaust trinity (Corruption/Dark Embrace/Feel No Pain) still exists but is much harder to assemble.

### Archetypes

#### 1. Vulnerable Build (NEW -- Highest Priority)
The Ironclad can exploit Vulnerability in ways other characters cannot, gaining card draw, block, and other bonuses when enemies are Vulnerable.

**Core Cards:**
- **Bash** (starter): Applies Vulnerable. Upgrade for more Vulnerable turns.
- **Tremble**: 0-cost Vulnerable application.
- **Bully**: Scales damage directly with turns of Vulnerability remaining on enemy.
- **Vicious** (Power): Whenever you apply Vulnerable, draw 1 card.
- **Hellraiser / Stampede**: Play more attacks for free.

**Strategy:** Apply Vulnerable early, then chain attacks that benefit from it. Vicious turns Vulnerable into a draw engine.

#### 2. Self-Damage / Strength Build
Rupture converts self-damage into Strength stacks. This is now top-tier in STS2.

**Core Cards:**
- **Rupture** (Power): Gain 1 Strength whenever you lose HP on your turn. Upgraded: 2 Strength.
- **Bloodletting**: Lose HP, gain energy. Key enabler.
- **Hemokinesis**: High damage + HP loss = Rupture fuel.
- **Offering**: Lose HP, gain energy + draw.
- **Inferno** (Power): At start of turn, lose 1 HP. Whenever you lose HP on your turn, deal damage to ALL enemies.
- **Spite**: Draw card if you lost HP this turn.
- **Blood Wall**: Converts HP into Block.

**Strategy:** Play Rupture ASAP, then chain self-damage cards. Stack multiple HP-loss cards in the same turn for explosive Strength scaling. High-risk, high-reward.

#### 3. Exhaust Build
Still viable but harder to assemble due to rarity changes.

**Core Cards:**
- **Dark Embrace** (Rare): Draw a card whenever a card is Exhausted.
- **Feel No Pain**: Gain Block on each Exhaust.
- **Corruption** (Ancient-only): All Skills cost 0 but Exhaust after play.
- **Fiend Fire / Cinder**: Mass exhaust enablers.

**Strategy:** The holy trinity of Corruption + Dark Embrace + Feel No Pain creates a loop where you burn through Skills rapidly, drawing constantly while building Block. Much harder to find all pieces in STS2.

#### 4. Block/Body Slam Build
Forgiving against heavy-hitting enemies.

**Core Cards:**
- **Body Slam**: Deals damage equal to your current Block. Goes nuclear late game.
- **Barricade** (Power): Block no longer decays at end of turn.
- **Entrench**: Double your current Block.
- **True Grit**: Block + Exhaust a random card.

**Strategy:** Stack Block over multiple turns with Barricade, then Body Slam for massive damage.

### Upgrade Priority
1. **Rupture** (if self-damage build -- doubles from 1 to 2 Strength per trigger)
2. **Bash** (more Vulnerable turns = more exploitable damage)
3. Key power cards (Demon Form, Vicious, etc.)
4. Block cards you use every combat

### Boss Fight Approach
- Ironclad's sustain (Burning Blood) gives him a natural advantage in attrition fights.
- Vulnerable application is key against bosses -- Bully + Vulnerable stacks = massive damage windows.
- Self-damage builds need careful HP management before boss fights. Rest before bosses when below 50% HP.
- Block builds are safest against high-damage bosses but slower to kill.

## The Silent

### Key Changes from STS1
- **New Sly keyword**: Cards with Sly are played for free when discarded. This is the strongest mechanic in STS2.
- Core Poison and Shiv builds remain but are refined with new card additions.
- **Accelerant** replaces Catalyst as the key Poison multiplier (Poison no longer decreases and increases by 1 each tick).
- Ring of the Snake still draws 2 extra cards at combat start.
- Can upgrade to Ring of the Serpent (Boss Relic): draw 1 extra card every turn instead.

### Archetypes

#### 1. Sly Discard Build (S+ TIER -- Strongest Build in STS2)
Cards with the Sly keyword play themselves for free when discarded from your hand during your turn. This creates turns where energy is irrelevant.

**Core Cards:**
- **Master Planner**: Turns every Skill into a self-playing combo piece via Sly. Core for discard builds.
- **Hidden Daggers**: Free discard that generates multiple Shivs.
- **Acrobatics**: Draw cards + discard, triggers Sly.
- **Prepared**: Discard to draw. Simple Sly trigger.
- **Dagger Throw**: Discard trigger + damage.
- **Tools of the Trade** (Power): Cleanest support -- turns every turn into a discard setup.

**Strategy:** Find discard outlets early, fill deck with Sly cards. Keep deck small (15-18 cards) so pieces show up together. Goal is cycling through entire arsenal in a single turn. Energy stops being the limiting factor.

#### 2. Poison Build
Stack Poison on enemies, then multiply it. Strong against bosses with large HP pools.

**Core Cards:**
- **Deadly Poison / Poisoned Stab**: Primary Poison stackers.
- **Noxious Fumes** (Power): Apply Poison to all enemies each turn.
- **Accelerant**: Poison no longer decreases and increases by 1 per tick. Game-winning against bosses.
- **Corpse Explosion**: When a poisoned enemy dies, deal Poison to all enemies.
- **Crippling Cloud**: AoE Poison + Weak.

**Weakness:** Artefact enemies counter Poison. Need direct damage backup or ways to burn through Artefact stacks.

**Strategy:** Stack Poison early, play Accelerant to lock in permanent scaling, then defend while Poison does the work.

#### 3. Shiv Build
Generate many 0-cost Shiv cards and make each one hit hard.

**Core Cards:**
- **Blade Dance**: Generates multiple Shivs into hand.
- **Infinite Blades** (Power): Create a Shiv each turn.
- **Accuracy** (Power): Doubles Shiv damage. Get as many copies as possible.
- **Afterimage** (Power): Gain 1 Block per card played. Shivs are 0-cost = free Block.

**Strategy:** Shiv builds rely heavily on relic synergy. Accuracy is the #1 signal to go Shivs. Afterimage provides massive free Block.

### Upgrade Priority
1. **Neutralize** (highest priority -- upgraded gives free 2 Weak for 0 energy)
2. **Footwork** (2->3 Dexterity per copy, huge defensive scaling)
3. **Noxious Fumes** (if Poison build)
4. Power cards first, then key skills.

### Early Game (Act 1) Strategy
- **Top priority**: Efficient Block cards and Weak application. You CANNOT heal (no Burning Blood equivalent).
- Do NOT fill deck with Poison cards in Act 1 -- you won't survive without Block.
- Draft Dash, Backflip, Footwork before committing to Poison.
- **Wraith Form** is game-winning: 2-3 turns of Intangible makes you nearly invincible.
- **Well-Laid Plans**: Retains key cards between turns for reliable combo setup.

### Boss Fight Approach
- Poison builds excel at bosses due to sustained damage scaling.
- Sly builds can burst down bosses in 1-2 turns with the right setup.
- Wraith Form is your panic button -- save it for boss burst turns.
- Footwork stacking makes long fights survivable.

## The Defect

### Key Changes from STS1
- **Focus is now mostly temporary** -- gains apply for current turn then revert. This fundamentally changes the Defect from STS1.
- **Defragment** moved from Uncommon to Rare.
- **Biased Cognition** removed from standard card pool -- only available as Ancient reward.
- **New Glass Orb type**: AoE damage, ticks down from 4 each turn, disappears if not Evoked.
- **Dark Orbs buffed**: More support cards, Consuming Shadow Channels 2 Dark + auto-Evokes.
- Gameplay shifted from slow snowball to dynamic turn-by-turn play.

### Orb Mechanics (STS2)

| Orb Type | Passive (per turn) | Evoke (one-time) | Affected by Focus? |
|----------|-------------------|-------------------|-------------------|
| Lightning | Deal 3+Focus damage to random enemy | Deal 8+Focus damage to random enemy | Yes |
| Frost | Gain 2+Focus Block | Gain 5+Focus Block | Yes |
| Dark | Add 6+Focus to stored damage | Deal stored damage to lowest-HP enemy | Yes |
| Plasma | Generate 1 Energy at turn start | Generate 2 Energy | No |
| Glass (NEW) | AoE damage, ticks down each turn | AoE burst before disappearing | Yes |

**Orb Slots**: Start with 3. When all slots are full, channeling a new orb Evokes the rightmost orb first.

**Focus**: Increases passive and Evoke values for all orbs except Plasma. In STS2, most Focus gains are TEMPORARY (apply for current turn only).

### Archetypes

#### 1. Focus/Orb Build (Primary)
The core Defect strategy. Maximize Focus to amplify all orbs.

**Core Cards:**
- **Defragment** (Rare): Permanent Focus gain. Upgrade priority #1.
- **Coolheaded**: Best Common -- provides Frost Orb + card draw + sets up Evokes.
- **Glacier**: 2 Frost Orbs + Block in one card. Excellent defensive option.
- **Capacitor**: Increase Orb slots. More slots = more passive value.
- **Storm** (Power): Whenever you play a Power, Channel a Lightning Orb.
- **Consuming Shadow**: Channel 2 Dark Orbs + auto-Evoke leftmost at end of turn.

**Strategy:** Fewer orb slots = faster Evokes and more Lightning damage. More slots = Dark Orbs grow longer and Frost stacks more Block. Choose based on build direction. Prioritize Focus cards above all else.

#### 2. Claw Deck (Alternative)
A spammy 0-cost attack deck that ignores orbs entirely.

**Core Cards:**
- **Claw**: 0-cost attack. Damage increases permanently each time played in combat.
- **Scrape / FTL / Beam Cell / Go for the Eyes**: 0-cost or cheap support.
- **Hologram**: Retrieve cards from discard.
- **All for One**: Plays all 0-cost cards from discard.

**Strategy:** Does NOT use orbs at all. Cycle through deck fast, spam Claw for exponentially growing damage. Evaluate relic offers carefully -- orb relics are useless.

#### 3. Dark Orb Build
High single-target burst damage via Dark Orbs charging over multiple turns.

**Core Cards:**
- **Doom and Gloom**: Channel Dark Orb.
- **Consuming Shadow**: Channel 2 Dark + auto-Evoke. Standout card.
- Dark Orbs start at 6 damage, gain 6+Focus per turn. With Focus, they become massive finishers.

**Strategy:** Let Dark Orbs charge while defending with Frost Orbs. Evoke Dark Orbs at the right moment for massive single-target damage to lowest-HP enemy.

### Upgrade Priority
1. **Defragment** (doubles Focus gain, amplifies entire orb engine)
2. **Coolheaded** (better draw + more Frost value)
3. **Biased Cognition** (if obtained from Ancient)
4. **Echo Form** (playing best card twice per turn is insurmountable)
5. Focus and orb-generating cards

### Early Game Strategy
- Set up orbs early -- passive effects compound over time.
- Cracked Core gives immediate Lightning damage turn 1.
- Prioritize Focus early: even small Focus increases amplify everything.
- Coolheaded is the best Common -- always take it.

### Boss Fight Approach
- Biased Cognition (if available) is a "finish in 4 turns" button.
- Echo Form doubles your best card each turn -- insane for long fights.
- Frost Orbs + Focus = enormous Block generation for survival.
- Dark Orbs are the best single-target finishers against high-HP bosses.

## The Regent

### Overview
The Regent is a NEW character in STS2, widely considered the strongest character in the current meta. Uses a dual resource system: Energy (standard) + Stars (unique secondary resource).

### Star Mechanic
- Stars are a secondary resource separate from Energy.
- Stars do NOT reset at end of turn -- they persist across turns.
- No cap on how many Stars you can hold.
- Combat starts with 3 Stars (from Divine Right relic).
- Stars are generated by specific cards and spent by payoff cards.
- **Upgraded Divine Right** gives 6 Stars at combat start (extremely strong).

### Archetypes

#### 1. Star Build (PRIMARY -- Strongest)
Generate Stars, bank them, then spend in massive combo turns.

**Star Generators (pick 2-3 early):**
- **Genesis** (Power): Gain 2 Stars at start of each turn. Best overall Star generator.
- **Glow**: Produces a Star + adds 2 cards to hand. Excellent early game.
- **Hidden Cache**: 1 Energy for 5 Stars (absurd value). Setup tool.
- **Royal Gamble**: Massive Star gain in one play.
- **Venerate** (starter): Basic Star generation.

**Star Payoff Cards:**
- **Gamma Blast**: Huge damage scaling with Stars spent.
- **Seven Stars**: Multi-hit damage payoff.
- **Stardust**: 5 damage per Star spent (7 upgraded, 10 with Miniature Cannon relic).
- **Comet**: Cheap high damage + Weak + Vulnerable. Strong once Star pool is high.
- **Bombardment**: Repeated damage through exhaustion. Lethal in long fights.
- **Big Bang**: Hits every major mechanic in one card. Best utility card.

**Defense:**
- **Reflect**: Excellent defense that punishes enemy attacks.
- **Bullwalk / I'm Invincible**: Cheap Block options.
- **Convergence**: Retains key cards + grants Stars and Energy next turn.

**Strategy:**
1. Generate Stars for 1-2 turns.
2. Build to 8+ Stars.
3. Spend all Stars in one massive payoff turn.
4. Target deck size of 15-18 cards for consistency.

#### 2. Forge / Sovereign Blade Build
Permanently upgrade the Sovereign Blade weapon throughout combat.

**Core Cards:**
- **Sovereign Blade**: 2-Energy weapon with Retain. Gains permanent damage from Forge cards.
- **Wrought in War**: Deals damage + Forges 5. Replace starter Strikes with this.
- **Bulwark**: 13 Block + Forges 10. Defensive anchor.
- **Conqueror** (Rare): Forges 3 + DOUBLES Sovereign Blade damage for the turn.
- **Know Thy Place**: Apply Vulnerable for free. Use after Conqueror for one-shot kills.

**Strategy:**
1. Do NOT play Sovereign Blade until damage reaches 30-50+.
2. Stack Forge each turn while surviving with Block cards.
3. Drop Conqueror to double Blade damage, apply Vulnerable, then swing for lethal.

### CRITICAL: Do Not Mix Builds
If you try to build Stars AND Forge simultaneously, you get a bloated deck that fails at both. **Commit to one win condition early.**

### Upgrade Priority
1. **Divine Right** relic upgrade (3 -> 6 starting Stars) -- highest priority if offered as Boss Relic
2. Star generators (Genesis, Glow)
3. Block cards (Bullwalk)
4. Payoff cards last (they're already strong unupgraded)

### Early Game (Act 1)
- Draft priorities: Reliable early damage, 1-2 Star generators, at least 1 solid Block option.
- Gather Light and Solar Strike are good Common pickups.
- Hidden Cache is excellent setup.
- Do NOT chase scaling in Act 1 -- survive first.
- Commit to Star OR Forge by mid-Act 1.

### Boss Fight Approach
- Star builds: Bank Stars for 2-3 turns, then unload everything in one massive payoff turn.
- Forge builds: Stall with Block while scaling Sovereign Blade, then one-shot the boss.
- Regent has comfortable 75 HP and strong scaling -- can usually race bosses.

## The Necrobinder

### Overview
The Necrobinder is a NEW character in STS2. A fragile lich who fights alongside Osty, a reanimated skeletal hand. Lowest HP in the game (66). Three unique mechanics: Osty/Summon, Souls, and Doom.

### Osty / Summon Mechanic
- Osty is summoned at combat start via Bound Phylactery relic.
- Osty starts with 1 HP each combat.
- **Summon X** increases Osty's Max HP by X (permanent for the fight).
- Osty intercepts attacks aimed at you, acting as a shield.
- If Osty dies, he reassembles on your next turn (with 1 HP).
- Osty can attack independently even if you're debuffed.
- Osty's HP is NOT Block -- it doesn't decay.

### Souls Mechanic
- Souls are colorless, 0-Energy cards that draw 2 cards and Exhaust when played.
- Created by other Necrobinder cards (never generated naturally).
- Enable massive card cycling -- in a deck of 10 cards + 8 Souls, you play your entire deck every turn.
- Synergize with payoff cards that trigger on Soul play.

### Doom Mechanic
- Doom is a stacking debuff on enemies.
- When an enemy's HP is at or below their Doom stacks at the END of the enemy's turn, they are instantly killed (executed).
- Doom ignores Block once the threshold is met.
- Requires patience -- you must wait until enemy's turn end for execution.

### Archetypes

#### 1. Soul Build (Most Consistent)
Create Souls, cycle through deck rapidly, trigger payoff effects.

**Soul Generators:**
- **Grave Warden**: Makes 1 Soul + Block. Incredible replacement for Defend cards.
- **Capture Spirit**: Damage + creates 3 Souls for 1 Energy.
- **Dirge**: Makes X Souls + increases Osty's HP. Defense setup.
- **Seance / Severance**: Additional Soul sources.

**Soul Payoff Cards:**
- **Haunt** (Power): Deal 6 damage to random enemy whenever you play a Soul. Turns Souls into a machine gun.
- **Devour Life** (Power): Defend yourself + deal damage just by playing Souls.
- **Soul Storm**: Finisher card -- massive damage after playing many Souls.

**Strategy:** Thin non-Soul cards through Exhaust, generate more Souls each turn, creating a positive feedback loop. Goal is to play 8+ Souls per turn with Haunt active.

#### 2. Osty / Summon Build (Safest)
Buff Osty into a boss-level companion. Best for new Necrobinder players.

**Core Cards:**
- **Squeeze**: Osty deals 25+ damage, +5 per Osty attack card in deck. Massive scaling.
- **Flatten**: Free Osty attack (0 cost if you played an Osty attack first).
- **Rattle**: Multi-attack source with combo potential.
- **Snap**: Cheap Osty attack with Retain.
- **Pull Aggro**: 11 Block value + Summon synergy.
- **Bodyguard** (starter): Basic Summon card.
- **Fetch**: Enables Flatten's free play condition.

**Key Relic:** Bone Flute -- gain 2 Block whenever Osty attacks. Adds up extremely fast.

**Strategy:** Summon early and often to build Osty's HP. Use Osty as a damage sponge while you set up. Squeeze scales with deck composition -- the more Osty attack cards, the harder it hits.

#### 3. Doom Build (Highest Ceiling, Hardest to Execute)
Stack Doom to execute enemies regardless of their Block or remaining HP.

**Core Cards:**
- **Scourge**: Foundation card. Applies Doom + replaces itself. Bypasses Block.
- **No Escape**: Apply 10 Doom + 5 more per 10 stacks already present. Exponential scaling.
- **Death's Door**: Block that scales with Doom application (up to 24 Block upgraded).
- **End of Days**: Solves the "waiting for enemy turn" problem.
- **Time's Up**: Deal damage equal to Doom on enemy. Burst without waiting for execution.
- **Shroud**: Converts Doom triggers into Block.

**Weakness:** Requires full unlock pool, expensive cards, slow to set up. Needs Osty to tank damage during setup.

**Key Relics:**
- Undying Sigil: Targets with Doom >= HP deal 50% less damage.
- Book Repair Knife: Heal 3 HP when a non-minion enemy dies to Doom.

### Upgrade Priority
1. **Soul Spark** (starter damage card) -- first upgrade. Goes from 6 to 9 damage + applies 1 Vulnerable. Critical for Act 1 elites.
2. Grave Warden / Capture Spirit (Soul generators)
3. Haunt (if Soul build)
4. Squeeze (if Osty build)

### Early Game (Act 1)
- First priority: 2-3 solid attack cards + 1 Vulnerable source.
- Low-cost exhaust enablers > minion summoning in Act 1.
- Good early picks: Pull Aggro, Snap, High Five (AoE + Vulnerable), Rattle.
- **"Swarm" build is a trap in Act 1** unless you find Necromancer's Crown boss relic.
- Prioritizing exhaust enablers over summons increases Act 1 win rate by ~40%.

### Boss Fight Approach
- Soul builds: Cycle through deck multiple times, Haunt triggers stack up to massive damage.
- Osty builds: Let Osty tank while you set up, then burst with Squeeze + Flatten.
- Doom builds: Stack Doom early, then use Time's Up or End of Days to bypass the waiting mechanic.
- Always keep Osty alive for damage absorption -- the Necrobinder's 66 HP cannot take direct hits.

## General Strategy Tips

### Deck Building Principles
1. **Do NOT force a predetermined build.** Read what the Spire offers in card rewards and relics. Stay flexible through Act 1, commit to an archetype once you have enough pieces.
2. **Target deck size of 20-25 cards** by Act 1 boss. Every card added dilutes draw pool.
3. **Card removal is extremely valuable.** Remove Strikes and Defends at shops -- this does more for win rate than adding mediocre cards.
4. **Skip card rewards** when no option strengthens your existing direction.
5. **Upgrade > Add** in most situations. Upgraded cards deal higher damage, cost less, or generate additional effects.

### Card Evaluation Framework
- A card that functions independently (without requiring another specific card) is ALWAYS safer to draft early.
- Before chasing a win condition, ensure: reliable damage, consistent block, at least one answer to high-attack enemies.
- Specialization only becomes worthwhile once foundation is in place.

### Upgrade Strategy
- Upgrade at Campfires (Smith) at least twice per run through deliberate pathing.
- Apotheosis (if found) upgrades every card in hand -- highest priority pick.
- Power cards generally benefit most from upgrades (permanent value).
- Upgrade damage cards that you play every combat.

### Shop Strategy
- Card removal > card purchase in most cases.
- Relics compound across the full run -- worth prioritizing over individual card buys.
- Buy relics when gold allows, especially energy relics.

## Boss Guide

### Act 1a -- Overgrowth
| Boss | Key Mechanic | Strategy |
|------|-------------|----------|
| Ceremonial Beast | Multi-phase fight | Focus damage during vulnerable phases |
| Kin Priest | Heals and buffs | Burst damage to prevent healing |
| Vantom | Slippery debuff + Turn 3 spike (25+ damage) | Clear Slippery with cheap attacks T1-2. Save Block for Turn 3 spike |

### Act 1b -- Underdocks
| Boss | Key Mechanic | Strategy |
|------|-------------|----------|
| Lagavulin Matriarch | Debuffs + heavy attacks | Don't let debuffs stack. Aggressive play. |
| Soul Fysh | Multiple phases | Manage phases carefully |
| Waterfall Giant | High HP pool | Sustained damage or Poison |

### Act 2 -- Hive
| Boss | Key Mechanic | Strategy |
|------|-------------|----------|
| Kaiser Crab | Very tanky | Poison is universal cheese. Stack DOT effects. |
| Knowledge Demon | Permanent debuffs + self-heal | Need either heavy defense OR heavy offense. Combo decks excel. |
| The Insatiable | Sandpit mechanic (timer to death), adds 6 Frantic Escape cards | Keep deck under 20 cards. Draw Frantic Escapes reliably. |

### Act 3 -- Glory
| Boss | Key Mechanic | Strategy |
|------|-------------|----------|
| Doormaker | Multi-phase | Silent/Regent handle well (Poison carryover) |
| Queen | Complex attack patterns | Requires fully scaled build with clear win condition |
| Test Subject | Final boss | Need complete deck. All scaling should be online. |

### General Boss Tips
- Every boss follows a predictable cycle. Identify which turns demand Block vs. free damage windows.
- Act 1 tests fundamentals. Act 2 punishes passive play. Act 3 requires fully scaled builds.
- Save potions for boss fights -- they're your emergency buttons.
- Rest before bosses when below ~60% HP (character dependent).
